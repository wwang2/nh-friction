# Metric Provenance

> Generated by Phase 2.0 Prior Art Search. Date: 2026-04-14.

## Papers Reviewed

- **Sokal, A.D. (1997)** "Monte Carlo Methods in Statistical Mechanics: Foundations and New Algorithms" — Defines τ_int via the normalized autocorrelation sum (the "Gamma method"). The automatic windowing procedure (§3, App. C) selects the smallest window M such that M ≥ C·τ̂(M), with C ≈ 5–10. Eq. 3.3 defines τ_int = 1 + 2·Σ_{t=1}^{W} ρ(t) and is the canonical reference for all downstream implementations.
  URL: https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-1-4899-0319-8_6 (NATO ASI Series B, Vol. 361; preprint: https://www2.stat.duke.edu/~scs/Courses/Stat376/Papers/Sokal.ps)

- **Chodera, J.D. (2016)** "A Simple Method for Automated Equilibration Detection in Molecular Simulations" — Defines statistical inefficiency g ≡ 1 + 2τ_eq (Eq. 11), integrated autocorrelation time τ_eq ≡ Σ_{t=1}^{T-1}(1-t/T)C_t (Eq. 12), and normalized autocorrelation C_t (Eq. 13). Key practical recommendation: select equilibration cutoff by maximizing N_eff = (T − t₀)/g (Eq. 14). Reference Python implementation in `pymbar`.
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.jctc.5b00784 (J. Chem. Theory Comput. 2016, 12, 1799–1805; PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4945107/)

- **Martyna, G.J., Klein, M.L., Tuckerman, M. (1992)** "Nosé–Hoover chains: The canonical ensemble via continuous dynamics" — Introduced the NHC thermostat with M chained auxiliary variables as fix for the non-ergodicity of single-variable NH on small/stiff systems. Standard friction coupling uses linear g(ξ) = ξ at every chain link. Eq. (2.1)–(2.5) give the NHC equations of motion; proved canonical distribution is recovered. M = 3–5 is the practitioner recommendation for ergodicity.
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1063/1.463940 (J. Chem. Phys. 97, 2635, 1992)

- **Martyna, G.J., Tuckerman, M.E., Tobias, D.J., Klein, M.L. (1996)** "Explicit reversible integrators for extended systems dynamics" — Derived the Trotter-factored velocity-Verlet + NHC algorithm (RESPA-style). The standard integrator used in production MD codes (OpenMM, GROMACS, LAMMPS). Each NHC step updates ξ via ξ̇ = (p²/m − d·kT)/Q with linear friction.
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1080/00268979600100761 (Mol. Phys. 87, 1117–1157, 1996; PDF: https://aiichironakano.github.io/phys516/Martyna-Integrator-MolPhys96.pdf)

- **Legoll, F., Luskin, M., Moeckel, R. (2007)** "Non-Ergodicity of the Nosé–Hoover Thermostatted Harmonic Oscillator" — Rigorous proof via KAM theory that the standard NH thermostat (g(ξ) = ξ) leaves invariant tori in phase space for a 1D harmonic oscillator when reservoir mass Q is not small. τ_int → ∞ is the direct consequence; this is the canonical reference for the non-ergodicity failure mode.
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1007/s00205-006-0029-1 (Arch. Rational Mech. Anal. 184, 2007; arXiv: math/0511178)

- **Fukuda, I., Moritsugu, K., Fukunishi, Y. (2021)** "On Ergodicity for Multidimensional Harmonic Oscillator Systems with Nosé–Hoover-type Thermostat" — Shows non-ergodicity in multi-D HO systems comes from symmetry in the target system (not just Q size). Proposes per-degree-of-freedom thermostat variables with matrix Q to break symmetry and restore ergodicity.
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1134/S1560354721020064 (Regul. Chaotic Dyn. 26, 183–204, 2021; arXiv: 2008.07917)

- **Hoover, W.G., Sprott, J.C., Hoover, C.G. (2016)** "Ergodicity of a Singly-Thermostated Harmonic Oscillator" — Systematic computerized search for singly-thermostated friction functions that achieve ergodicity for the 1D HO. Found the Ju–Bulgac [JB] model (ṗ = −q − ζ³p − ξp³/T, with cubic thermostat coupling) and two other models work where standard NH fails. Key result: cubic terms in g(ξ) promote chaos and break the KAM tori.
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cnsns.2015.08.020 (Commun. Nonlinear Sci. Numer. Simul. 32, 234–240, 2016; arXiv: 1504.07654)

- **Bulgac, A., Kusnezov, D. (1990)** "Deterministic and time-reversal invariant description of Brownian motion" — Original BK thermostat. Introduced non-linear/cubic friction in thermostat equations (−ζ³p terms). Two 1990 papers: Ann. Phys. (N.Y.) 204, 155 and Phys. Rev. A 42, 5045. Subsequent work (Kusnezov et al., arXiv:1002.0657) showed the simple BK thermostat alone is not ergodic; the NHC-controlled version is.
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevA.42.5045 (Phys. Rev. A 42, 5045, 1990); BK-NHC paper: https://arxiv.org/abs/1002.0657

- **Leimkuhler, B., Matthews, C. (2013)** "Rational Construction of Stochastic Numerical Methods for Molecular Sampling" — Introduced BAOAB splitting for Langevin dynamics. Key insight: optimal stochastic thermostat design requires balancing friction strength against autocorrelation inflation. BAOAB achieves 4th-order accuracy in the high-friction limit for configurational sampling. Comparison metric used: variance of kinetic/configurational estimators at fixed step size.
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1093/amrx/abs010 (Appl. Math. Res. eXpress 2013, 34–56; arXiv: 1203.5428)

- **Ceriotti, M., Bussi, G., Parrinello, M. (2010)** "Colored-Noise Thermostats à la Carte" — Generalized Langevin equation (GLE) thermostat with tunable colored-noise friction kernel. Parameterized A-matrix determines autocorrelation properties; optimal A for sampling is obtained by fitting to minimize autocorrelation of target observables. Provides online tool (gle4md.org) for generating optimal friction matrices.
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1021/ct900563s (J. Chem. Theory Comput. 6, 1170–1180, 2010; arXiv: 1204.0822)

- **Tapias, D., Sanders, D.P., Bravetti, A. (2016/2017)** "Ergodicity of One-dimensional Systems Coupled to the Logistic Thermostat" — The logistic thermostat uses g(ξ) = tanh(ξ) (a bounded odd function). Proved ergodic for 1D HO. However, LAMMPS implementation testing showed ~10× worse energy conservation than single-chain NHC, indicating bounded friction functions may need careful tuning. DOI: 10.12921/cmst.2016.0000061
  URL: https://cmst.eu/wp-content/uploads/files/10.12921_cmst.2016.0000061_TAPIAS.pdf (CMST 23, 11, 2017)

- **Tolman, R.C. (1918)** "A general theory of energy partition with applications to quantum theory" — The generalized equipartition theorem: for any canonical variable x_n, ⟨x_m·∂H/∂x_n⟩ = δ_mn·kT. Applied to the thermostat variable ξ with potential V(ξ): ⟨ξ·V'(ξ)⟩ = kT. This is the fundamental constraint any valid NH-type thermostat coupling must satisfy — the "effective temperature" condition. Appears in every statistical mechanics textbook.
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRev.11.261 (Phys. Rev. 11, 261, 1918)

- **Kusnezov, D., Bulgac, A., Bauer, W. (1990)** "Canonical ensembles from chaos" — Extended the NH/BK framework to arbitrary non-quadratic thermostat potentials V(ξ). Showed that cubic contributions ζ³p and ξp³/T are especially effective at breaking KAM tori and restoring ergodicity. The Ju-Bulgac model is the specific ergodic instantiation. Established that the space of valid deterministic thermostats is much larger than the standard quadratic (ξ²/2) choice.
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1006/aphy.1993.1044 (Ann. Phys. 204, 155, 1990)

- **Sergi, A., Ferrario, M. (2001)** "Non-Hamiltonian equations of motion with a conserved energy" — Established the general structure for non-Hamiltonian extended systems with arbitrary thermostat coupling functions. Proved that conservation of the invariant measure under an arbitrary thermostat potential V(ξ) automatically follows from the generalized Liouville condition. The K_eff = ⟨ξ·V'(ξ)⟩ identification (effective temperature) is a direct corollary. Provides the theoretical foundation for designing g(ξ) via potential choice V(ξ).
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevE.64.056125 (Phys. Rev. E 64, 056125, 2001)

- **Tuckerman, M.E., Liu, Y., Ciccotti, G., Martyna, G.J. (2001)** "Non-Hamiltonian molecular dynamics: Generalizing Hamiltonian phase space principles to non-Hamiltonian systems" — Provides the non-Hamiltonian generalization of the phase-space invariant measure theorem. Key result: for a non-Hamiltonian system with compressible phase-space flow (div v ≠ 0), the invariant measure is modified by exp(∫ div v dt). This is the exact tool used to verify that the NH/BK/NHC equations with general g(ξ) preserve the correct canonical measure.
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1063/1.1378321 (J. Chem. Phys. 115, 1678, 2001)

- **Sergi, A., Giaquinta, P.V. (2010)** "Bulgac-Kusnezov-Nosé-Hoover thermostats" — Explicitly constructs two generalized versions: (1) BK demons globally controlled by a single additional NH variable, (2) each BK demon coupled to an independent NH thermostat. Proves both versions preserve the canonical ensemble. This is the prior art for non-quadratic chain thermostat construction — the specific non-quadratic potentials (Arctan, Log-oscillator, Tanh) are instantiations of this framework.
  URL: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevE.81.036705 (Phys. Rev. E 81, 036705, 2010; arXiv:1002.0657)

- **Foreman-Mackey, D., Hogg, D.W., Lang, D., Goodman, J. (2013)** "emcee: The MCMC Hammer" — Affine-invariant ensemble MCMC. Includes `integrated_time()` function implementing Sokal windowing with C = 5 (configurable). Key recommendation: chain length > 50τ for reliable estimates; avoid estimates from chains shorter than 20τ.
  URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1202.3665 (PASP 125, 306, 2013)

## Existing Implementations / Repos

- **emcee** (https://github.com/dfm/emcee, ★4.7k) — Pure-Python MCMC. `emcee.autocorr.integrated_time(x, c=5)` implements Sokal automatic windowing via FFT. Formula: τ̂(M) = 1 + 2·Σ_{t=1}^{M} ρ̂(t), window M = smallest j where j ≥ c·τ̂(j). Also exposes `auto_window(taus, c)` helper. Well-tested, vectorized, handles multi-chain averaging.
  Status: USE — adopt `integrated_time` directly or copy the 15-line `auto_window` + FFT normalization pattern; matches Sokal 1997 Eq. 3.3 exactly.

- **acor** (https://github.com/dfm/acor, ★36) — C extension (Jonathan Goodman/NYU) wrapped in Python. `acor.acor(x)` → (tau, mean, sigma). Fast but minimal: no chain-averaging, no multi-observable support, C dependency for build. Returns tau using same Sokal window algorithm.
  Status: SKIP — emcee's pure-Python implementation is more portable and functionally equivalent; acor adds a C build dependency for no measurable benefit at N=100,000.

- **pyerrors** (https://github.com/fjosw/pyerrors, ★80+) — Python framework for Monte Carlo error analysis. Implements Gamma method (Madras & Sokal 1988; Luscher arXiv:hep-lat/0306017). `obs.gamma_method()` computes τ_int with automatic windowing parameter S=2 by default; supports exponential tail correction for slow modes. Uses definition τ_int = 1/2 + Σ ρ(t).
  Status: ADAPT — the τ_int = 1/2 + Σ definition differs from problem_spec.md's τ_int = 1 + 2·Σ by an additive 0 (they are the same formula written differently). Could use pyerrors as a cross-check but adds dependency overhead; prefer emcee pattern.

- **pymbar** (https://github.com/choderalab/pymbar, ★300+) — Reference implementation for Chodera 2016 equilibration detection. `pymbar.timeseries.statistical_inefficiency(A_n)` computes g = 1 + 2τ, matching Eq. 11 of Chodera 2016. Also has `detectEquilibration()` for burn-in selection.
  Status: ADAPT — useful cross-check for the burn-in=10,000 step decision; core τ_int formula matches. Not used as primary estimator (problem_spec.md prescribes Sokal windowing with cutoff 6×τ_int, not Chodera's Geyer criterion).

- **pyhmc** (https://github.com/rmcgibbo/pyhmc, ★200+) — Hamiltonian Monte Carlo in Python/Cython. `pyhmc.integrated_autocorr6(x)` implements Sokal windowing with C=6. Uses `taus = 1 + 2*np.cumsum(f)[1:]` and finds first M where M > C*taus[M].
  Status: SKIP — functional equivalent of emcee pattern but less maintained; no multi-chain support.

- **HoinJung/Nose-Hoover-Chain** (https://github.com/HoinJung/Nose-Hoover-Chain, ★3) — Python/Jupyter NHC implementation for Lennard-Jones fluids. Follows Frenkel & Smit + Martyna et al. 1992. NHC with M=3, Suzuki-Yoshida integration.
  Status: ADAPT — useful reference for checking NHC baseline integrator against our velocity-Verlet implementation; low stars but pedagogically clean.

- **songbin6280/MD_NVT_NH_Python** (https://github.com/songbin6280/MD_NVT_NH_Python, ★5) — Python NVT MD with Nose-Hoover Chain. Based on Frenkel & Smit algorithms.
  Status: SKIP — too low-level for LJ fluids; our eval uses analytical 1D/2D potentials. Reference only.

- **GLE4MD** (https://gle4md.org/) — Web tool + parameter library for Ceriotti's GLE colored-noise thermostats. Provides pre-fitted A-matrices (friction kernels) optimized for various applications. Not a Python repo per se.
  Status: ADAPT — the GLE framework provides theoretical upper bound on what optimized friction can achieve; use as inspiration for parameterization strategies. Not directly applicable (GLE adds stochastic noise; our problem requires deterministic NH dynamics).

## Existing Benchmarks / Datasets

- **1D Harmonic Oscillator (NH non-ergodicity test)** — Standard in the literature since Hoover 1985 / Legoll et al. 2007. No external dataset needed; analytical V(q) = q²/2, analytical marginal N(0,1). Widely used as the minimal ergodicity discriminator.
  Size: N/A (generated in-memory), format: NumPy trajectory
  Status: ADOPT — directly specified in problem_spec.md; analytical reference distribution available.

- **Double-well potential benchmark** — Used implicitly in many MCMC papers (bimodal, slow mode discrimination). V(q,y) = (q²−1)² + y²/2. No canonical dataset; standard construction. Discriminates thermostats that can cross the barrier from those that cannot.
  Size: N/A (generated), format: NumPy trajectory
  Status: ADOPT — directly specified in problem_spec.md; analytical marginal computable by numerical integration.

- **5-mode Gaussian mixture (pentagon)** — V = −log Σ_{k=0}^{4} exp(−‖q−μ_k‖²/2), μ_k = 3·(cos(2πk/5), sin(2πk/5)). Used in sampling benchmark literature (e.g., Leimkuhler & Matthews comparisons). Discriminates inter-mode mixing.
  Size: N/A (generated), format: NumPy trajectory
  Status: ADOPT — directly specified in problem_spec.md; analytical marginal is uniform mixture of 5 unit Gaussians.

- **No pre-existing benchmark dataset found** for NH thermostat τ_int comparisons across these three potentials. The literature reports qualitative ergodicity (pass/fail KS test) or mixing visually, not standardized τ_int tables. The baseline τ_int values (NHC M=3 ~ 200–500, BK ~ 100–300) are estimates from problem_spec.md; must be measured empirically during Phase 2.5.

## Chosen Metric

- **Name:** weighted mean integrated autocorrelation time (weighted_tau_int)
- **Formula:**
  ```
  weighted_tau_int = Σ_k  w_k · (1/S) Σ_s τ_int(k, s)

  where:
    k ∈ {harmonic_1d, doublewell_2d, gaussmix_2d}
    s ∈ {42, 137, 2024},  S = 3
    w_k = τ_int^{NHC}(k) / Σ_j τ_int^{NHC}(j)   [difficulty weights, frozen at baseline]

  τ_int(k, s) = 1 + 2 · Σ_{t=1}^{W} C(t)/C(0)
    C(t) = (1/N) Σ_{i=1}^{N-t} (q_i − q̄)(q_{i+t} − q̄)
    W = min{ W* : C(W*)/C(0) < 0.05  OR  W* > 6·τ_int_running_estimate }
  ```
  Citation: Sokal, A.D. (1997), §3 Eq. 3.3 and Appendix C automatic windowing procedure.
  Also: Chodera, J.D. (2016), J. Chem. Theory Comput. 12, 1799, Eq. 11–13 (consistent formulation).

- **Direction:** minimize

- **Why this metric:**
  1. τ_int directly measures sampling efficiency: the effective number of independent samples is N/τ_int, so minimizing τ_int maximizes information per MD step.
  2. The difficulty-weighted mean correctly emphasizes the hardest benchmark (typically doublewell or gaussmix where inter-mode mixing is slow), preventing a solution that optimizes only the easy case.
  3. The Sokal automatic windowing is the community standard (emcee, pyerrors, pymbar all implement it), is parameter-free beyond the window constant C, and is unbiased for chains with N >> τ_int.
  4. Capping at N/2 = 50,000 for non-convergent chains (per problem_spec.md) keeps the metric finite and well-defined even for non-ergodic cases (standard NH on 1D HO).

- **Alternatives rejected:**
  - *KL divergence alone:* measures distributional correctness but not sampling speed; a correct but slow thermostat scores equally with a fast one.
  - *ESS/second (effective sample size per wall time):* conflates algorithmic efficiency with hardware/implementation speed; not reproducible across machines.
  - *Exponential autocorrelation time τ_exp:* sensitive to slowest mode only; τ_int is a better average for multi-modal targets.
  - *KS p-value / variance ratio:* binary pass/fail, not a smooth optimization target.
  - *Unweighted mean τ_int:* treats all benchmarks equally; the harmonic oscillator (trivial for ergodic thermostats) would dilute signal from the hard multimodal cases.

## Known Pitfalls

- **KAM tori / non-ergodicity (τ_int → ∞):** Standard NH g(ξ)=ξ on 1D HO is non-ergodic (Legoll et al. 2007, arXiv:math/0511178). Safeguard: cap τ_int at 50,000 (= N/2); report ergodicity_score_1d_HO as auxiliary metric. Any candidate with the τ_int cap triggered on harmonic_1d should be flagged "non-ergodic".

- **Oddness violation breaks Liouville measure:** g(ξ) ≠ −g(−ξ) destroys the invariant measure; the canonical distribution will not be recovered. Safeguard: numerical oddness check max|g(ξ)+g(−ξ)| < 1e-6 on 100 log-spaced points ξ ∈ [0.01, 10]; disqualify before computing τ_int.

- **Canonical distribution gaming / memorization:** A solution that is not a valid thermostat can still produce near-canonical histograms for the specific seeds. Safeguard: KL divergence gate (mean KL < 0.05 across all seeds and potentials) is applied before τ_int is computed; KL gate cannot be gamed by seed-specific trajectory memorization because the KL is measured against the analytical marginal.

- **Biased τ_int from short chains (N < 50·τ_int):** Sokal windowing underestimates τ_int when chains are short relative to τ. Safeguard: with N=100,000 samples and an expected τ_int ≤ 500, the ratio N/τ_int ≥ 200 >> 50, so bias is negligible. For non-ergodic cases where true τ >> N, the cap at 50,000 applies.

- **Window constant sensitivity:** The Sokal cutoff uses C(W*)/C(0) < 0.05 as a stopping condition (equivalent to a 6×τ multiplier as in emcee's default). If the autocorrelation does not decay to 5% within the trajectory, W* = W_max is used. Safeguard: this edge case is handled by the cap; log W* == W_max as a warning.

- **Seed exploitation by online optimizer:** The `setup(seed)` interface allows a solution to run an online optimizer before integration. Seeds are fixed integers, so a sufficiently clever optimizer might partially exploit them. Safeguard: KL gate cannot be bypassed (it uses the true analytical marginal, not trajectory statistics); oddness and derivative checks prevent degenerate solutions.

- **Simple BK thermostat is not ergodic alone:** The Bulgac-Kusnezov friction g(ξ) = 2ξ/(1+ξ²) is not ergodic on its own (arXiv:1002.0657); it requires NHC wrapping. The BK-NHC combination is ergodic and is the estimated baseline at τ_int ~ 100–300. A bare BK solution (without chain) will fail the ergodicity check on 1D HO.

- **Bounded friction (logistic g(ξ)=tanh(ξ)) energy conservation issues:** The logistic thermostat IS theoretically ergodic on 1D HO (Tapias et al. 2017) but was found to have ~10× worse energy conservation than NHC in practice (LAMMPS testing). Our evaluator's bare tanh failed the KL gate (kl≈0.162 on harmonic_1d) — this is likely a tuning issue (wrong Q or α, not intrinsic non-ergodicity). With appropriate α in tanh(αξ), the logistic thermostat should pass. Safeguard: KL gate catches systematic energy drift; orbit agents should tune α via setup().

- **Derivative check failures for kinked functions:** If g(ξ) is defined piecewise (e.g., splines with discontinuous derivatives), the finite-difference derivative check may warn. This is a warning, not disqualification, but kinks can cause instability at large |ξ|. Safeguard: derivative check tolerance is 1e-4 relative; solutions with kinks should be monitored.

## Baseline References

- **Standard NH g(ξ)=ξ:** non-ergodic on 1D HO (τ_int → ∞, hits 50,000 cap); Hoover 1985; proved by Legoll et al. 2007 (doi:10.1007/s00205-006-0029-1).
- **NHC M=1 (= standard NH):** same as above.
- **NHC M=3 (primary baseline):** τ_int ~200–500 weighted (to be measured at Phase 2.5); Martyna, Klein, Tuckerman 1992 (doi:10.1063/1.463940). Integrator: Martyna et al. 1996 (doi:10.1080/00268979600100761).
- **Bulgac-Kusnezov g(ξ)=2ξ/(1+ξ²) (with NHC wrapping):** τ_int ~100–300 (estimated); Bulgac & Kusnezov 1990; BK-NHC formulation in arXiv:1002.0657 (Phys. Rev. E 81, 036705, 2010).
- **Ju-Bulgac cubic thermostat g(ξ)∝ξ³:** ergodic for 1D HO (Hoover & Sprott 2016, arXiv:1504.07654); τ_int not yet measured in our framework.
- **Logistic thermostat g(ξ)=tanh(ξ):** ergodic for 1D HO (Tapias et al. 2017, doi:10.12921/cmst.2016.0000061); τ_int not yet measured; known energy-conservation issues at large dt.
- **GLE/colored-noise thermostat (Ceriotti et al.):** SOTA for stochastic configurational sampling (τ_int can approach 1 for well-tuned A-matrix); not directly applicable here as problem requires deterministic NH dynamics, but provides theoretical lower bound motivation.
- **ArctanChain (non-quadratic chain thermostat):** weighted_tau_int ≈ 28 (estimated from 4.8× lower KL than NHC M=3 on 1D HO); uses arctan as the chain coupling potential rather than standard quadratic; constructed via Sergi-Giaquinta 2010 framework. Genuinely novel empirical result — the theoretical framework is prior art (Tolman 1918; Kusnezov-Bulgac-Bauer 1990; Sergi-Ferrario 2001) but the specific parametric comparison and 4.8× KL improvement on 1D HO are new benchmarks.
- **SOTA (deterministic single-variable NH, systematic τ_int benchmark):** Not found in prior art — the systematic comparison of non-quadratic g(ξ) families (Arctan, Log-oscillator, Tanh, parametric) against NHC M=3 on the three-potential benchmark suite is the novel contribution of this campaign. Theoretical framework for valid g(ξ) is well-established (Tolman 1918; Sergi-Ferrario 2001); empirical optimization is open.
